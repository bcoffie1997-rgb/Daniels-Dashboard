# 01 — STRATEGY

## Problem
Inventory at Daniel's, A Florida Steakhouse (Fort Lauderdale) is done in spreadsheets — slow, error-prone, no accountability for who counted what, disconnected from what actually sold. By the time numbers consolidate, decisions are reactive.

## Target user
- **Primary:** Line cooks, sous chefs, stewards who physically count (walk-ins, dry storage, bar, wine cellar)
- **Secondary:** Executive Chef / Chef de Cuisine / GM reviewing counts for purchasing decisions
- **Tertiary:** Wine and Beverage Director (Daniel Bishop) for the wine cellar specifically
- **Builder:** Branden — Daniel's staff, has direct line-level access

## Value proposition
**Count inventory on your phone in half the time, with every count tied to who did it and when — so chefs see real numbers in real time instead of waiting for a spreadsheet.**

## Success metrics (90 days from Stage 1 pilot)
1. **Adoption:** 80%+ of weekly inventory counts done in Mise (not spreadsheets) by week 8
2. **Speed:** Average count time per station cut by 40% vs. spreadsheet baseline
3. **Trust:** Chef de Cuisine references Mise data in ≥1 purchasing decision per week

## Competitive landscape
| Tool | Position | Why Mise has a niche |
|---|---|---|
| **MarketMan** | Full inventory + ordering + recipe costing | Heavy, expensive, chain-focused |
| **xtraCHEF (Toast)** | Invoice scan + inventory + Toast POS | Locks you into Toast; UX is fast-casual |
| **MarginEdge** | Invoice + inventory + P&L | Accounting-first, chefs find it clunky |
| **Spreadsheets** | Free, flexible | No accountability, stale data, no mobile UX |

**The gap:** A kitchen-first, phone-first, fast counting tool. Linear-of-restaurant-inventory positioning, not SAP.

## Strategic fit
Standalone play with portfolio upside. Even if Mise only runs at Daniel's, it's a credentialed case study — *built and deployed in a Michelin-recommended kitchen.* Natural expansion: independent fine-dining and upscale restaurants — fragmented market underserved by incumbents. Data generated (count velocity, variance, waste patterns) is long-term moat material.

**Unfair advantage:** Branden has line-level access and operational understanding most restaurant-tech founders lack. Most founders building this kind of tool have never worked a line.

- Screenshots of the current spreadsheets being replaced
